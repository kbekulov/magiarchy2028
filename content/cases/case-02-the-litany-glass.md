# Case 02 - The Litany of Glass

The theatre insists the disturbances are artistic accidents.
The bureau has no category for artistic accidents that arrive before the event they describe.

Lines spoken in rehearsal begin to reappear in sworn statements three streets away.
Props develop marks that correspond to records not yet written.

## Bureau Concern

- If performance can seed testimony, then evidence can be staged before fact.
- If the chamber is listening through rehearsal, bureau procedure is already too slow.
- Sherie recommends controlled observation; Goat recommends letting the scene finish and seeing what it claims.
