# Truth Versus Stability

The story's central conflict is not moral simplicity. It is the cost of knowledge.

- Hidden systems preserve ordinary life by denying the public access to the truth.
- Institutions defend secrecy partly to protect civilization and partly to protect themselves.
- Revelation is dangerous not only because horrors exist, but because societies built on managed ignorance may not survive contact with the full structure beneath them.
