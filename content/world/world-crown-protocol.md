# Crown Protocol - Legitimacy and Seizure

The Crown is not everywhere.
It simply makes "everywhere" feel available.

Its power over incidents comes from narrative jurisdiction: the ability to decide
which account becomes public order, which becomes sealed doctrine, and which becomes treasonous imagination.

## Operational Consequence

- If the bureau fails to frame an incident first, the Crown reframes it for them.
- Chamber phenomena become political the moment they demand custody rather than explanation.
